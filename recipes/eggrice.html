Egg Fried Rice Recipe

Home

EEgg fried rice is the ultimate showcase of minimalist comfort food, relying on simple ingredients executed with flawless technique. At its core, this dish is all about the delicate, golden ribbons of scrambled egg that are gently folded into hot, fluffy rice. The eggs are typically whisked and cooked quickly in a hot wok so they stay incredibly soft, light, and pillowy, acting as a velvety contrast to the distinct, individual grains of rice.

Because the ingredient list is intentionally simple—usually rounded out with just a splash of light soy sauce, a pinch of white pepper, and a handful of freshly chopped scallions—the natural flavors really shine. The egg acts like a sponge, absorbing the subtle seasonings while lending its own rich, buttery sweetness to the dish. It is a brilliantly light yet satisfying meal that feels deeply nostalgic and effortlessly satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they are tender.
  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are fully cooked.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the skillet and stir everything together.
  5. Pour in the soy sauce and season with salt and pepper. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through. Serve hot and enjoy!
